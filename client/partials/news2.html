Lyoto Machida was the middleweight co-main event of UFC on Fox 19 against Dan Henderson, but has been removed from the card after Machida admitted to using a banned substance.

UFC released a statement on April 10th:

“Machida stated that he was unaware that the substance was prohibited both in and out of competition and, in accordance with the UFC Anti-Doping Policy, his disclosure of usage will be taken into consideration by USADA during any potential results management and adjudication process”

Lyoto released statement on April 13th:

I'm here to tell you that I was caught by surprise by this news. It has been very difficult for me. I've trained a lot for this fight, I've dedicated a lot with all my teammates,” Machida wrote. “I want to tell you that I really didn't know about this substance,

so I listed it on my declaration form amongst everything else I was taking, and this substance was recently banned in 2016, so I take the blame for not knowing that, and I'd like to say I'm really sorry and apologize to my fans, teammates, and my family... I always try to play by the book, but unfortunately this happened.”

Machida has lost three of his last four UFC fights, including a third-round technical knockout defeat to Yoel Romero at UFC Fight Night in Hollywood, Fla., this past June. He previously defeated Dan Henderson via split decision at UFC 157 in February 2013.

Conor Mcgregor's Teamate kills Joao “Rafeiro” Carvalho in Dublin match

Joao “Rafeiro” Carvalho

Portuguese welterweight Joao “Rafeiro” Carvalho has died after suffering a knockout loss at an MMA event in Dublin over the weekend.

Carvalho was stopped by Charlie Ward (Cono Mcgregor's teammate) in the third round of the fight at an April 9th event promoted by Irish organization Total Extreme Fighting (TEF).

TEF on Tuesday issued a statement via Facebook describing the aftermath of Carvalho’s bout.TEF stated that Carvalho passed away Monday night “after [two] days of the best medical care and treatment in Beaumont Hospital."

Carvalho’s coach, Vitor Nobrega said in a written statement:

“He was assessed immediately by the on-site medical team, and then quickly transported to Beaumont Hospital, where he underwent brain surgery, after which the athlete remained in critical condition for the next 48 hours.”
